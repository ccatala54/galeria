Welcome

In this gallery, various interpretations of mythology through art are displayed.

Here you can enjoy a small selection of works with different monthly themes, this month is:


MYTHOLOGY

We invite you to reflect on a universe where the divine and the human intertwine in shapes and colours, giving life to gods, heroes and creatures that have spanned centuries. Each piece is more than just a representation: it is an invocation to the invocation to the mystery, to the narrative power of myth, to the beauty that transcends time. For a brief moment, the mythology ceases to be legend to become presence. May this journey be a voyage between the sacred and the imagined, between the eternal and the ephemeral.

THE ABDUCTION OF EUROPA

Greek myth in which Zeus, fascinated by the beauty of the Phoenician princess Europa, transforms into a majestic white bull to approach her. When Europa climbs onto the animal’s back, Zeus carries her across the sea to the island of Crete. This scene symbolizes divine desire, the journey into the unknown, and the meeting of cultures, as from this union a new dynasty is born and the European continent gets its name.

PERSEUS WITH THE HEAD OF MEDUSA

A work that captures the hero Perseus after beheading Medusa, the gorgon whose gaze turned people to stone. More than a heroic victory, the image reflects the triumph of cleverness over terror, and the fascination with the monstrous as a symbol of power and danger.

DIANA AND ACTAEON

The scene shows the punishment of Actaeon, a hunter who accidentally sees the goddess Diana (Artemis) while she bathes. As punishment, she transforms him into a stag, and he is devoured by his own dogs. The work explores the fragile boundaries between the human and the divine, and the severity of the gods in the face of transgression.

APOLLO AND DAPHNE

A monument representing Apollo’s pursuit of Daphne, whom he falls in love with after being struck by Eros’s arrow. To escape, Daphne asks her father for help, who transforms her into a laurel tree just before being caught. The work captures the tension between desire and freedom, and the moment of transformation as a form of escape.

THE JUDGEMENT OF PARIS

Paris, a Trojan prince, must choose which of three goddesses—Hera, Athena, or Aphrodite—is the most beautiful. His decision, influenced by Aphrodite’s promise to grant him Helen’s love, triggers the Trojan War. This scene represents the mythical origin of the conflict, and the destructive power of beauty, desire, and human decisions.

THE RAPE OF PROSERPINA

Depicts the moment when Hades, god of the underworld, abducts Proserpina (Persephone in Greek mythology), daughter of the goddess Ceres, to make her queen of the underworld. This dramatic scene encompasses the eternal cycle of life and death, symbolizing the changing seasons: Ceres’s grief at losing her daughter brings about winter, while her return marks spring. The work captures both the violence of the abduction and the fragility of humanity in the face of divine forces.

ECHO AND NARCISSUS

Echo, a nymph cursed to repeat only the last words she hears, falls in love with Narcissus, who in turn falls in love with his own reflection. The work expresses the tragedy of unrequited love, narcissism, and the impossibility of true connection with another.

HECATE

Goddess of magic, crossroads, and the occult, Hecate is an enigmatic figure associated with the moon, night, and rituals. Often depicted with three faces or bodies, she embodies life’s cycles, ancient wisdom, and feminine power in its most arcane form.

HERCULES AND THE HYDRA

In one of his twelve labors, Hercules must defeat the Hydra of Lerna, a multi-headed monster whose heads regenerate when cut off. This scene represents the superhuman effort in the face of the impossible, the endless struggle against evil, and overcoming through cleverness and strength.
