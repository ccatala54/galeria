Welcome

In this gallery, various interpretations of death are displayed through art.

Here, you can enjoy a small selection of artworks with different monthly themes. This month’s theme is:


DEATH

We invite you to reflect on our relationship with death, the transience of life and the value of time. Through art, death is approached not as an end, but as an essential part of the cycle of existence, awakening in us a deeper awareness of our passage through the world. It questions how we live, what legacy we leave behind and how to face our inevitable departure with dignity and meaning.

THE DEATH OF MARAT

It depicts the French revolutionary Jean-Paul Marat murdered in his bathtub after being stabbed by Charlotte Corday. The work elevates Marat to the status of a political martyr. The subdued light, the limited palette and the serene pose of the body evoke Christian references, especially the iconography of the Pieta. The painting conveys a powerful message of sacrifice and revolutionary propaganda. and revolutionary propaganda, and became an iconic symbol of the French Revolution.
