Welcome

In this gallery, various interpretations of nature are displayed through art.

Here, you can enjoy a small selection of artworks with different monthly themes. This month’s theme is:


NATURE

We invite you to reflect on our relationship with the environment, the beauty of the landscapes and the fragility of the ecosystem. Through art, the importance of preserving nature is highlighted and environmental awareness is awakened in us , questioning how our actions impact the planet and what we can do to protect it.

LANDSCAPE WITH THE FALL OF ICARUS

It depicts an everyday scene in which peasants work the land, a boat sails and life goes on, while in an almost unnoticed corner, Icarus drowns after having fallen from the sky. The work underlines the indifference of nature and the world to individual tragedies, showing how personal suffering often goes unnoticed in the midst of daily routine.

GAIA

Large-scale sculpture of the Earth as seen from space, based on NASA images. Suspended in the air and slowly rotating, this work invites us to reflect on the beauty, fragility and interconnectedness of our planet.

More information

THE SCREAM

An anguished figure under a burning sky, amidst a distorted natural landscape. Inspired by a real-life experience during a sunset walk, Munch felt ‘an infinite scream piercing through nature’. The work expresses a deep existential anxiety, reflecting the connection between human emotions and the haunting force of the natural environment.

More information

SEED CATHEDRAL

Futuristic structure made up of 60,000 translucent rods containing seeds at their ends. It represents biodiversity and the importance of preserving nature, highlighting the potential of seeds as a symbol of life, growth and a sustainable future.

More information

WANDERER ABOVE THE SEA OF FOG

Man on his back, standing on a rocky peak, contemplating a landscape shrouded in clouds. The work symbolises the immensity of nature in front of the individual.

More information

THE FORCE OF NATURE

It is Mother Nature tossing or spinning the planet Earth with a moving canvas, as if it were a ball wrapped in wind. The work symbolises the unpredictable and overwhelming power of nature in the face of human control , reminding us of the fragility of humanity in the face of natural forces that we cannot control.

More information

WATER LILIES

It depicts the fleeting beauty of nature and the way light and colour transform the landscape. More than a simple image of a pond, the work reflects the tranquillity, the passage of time and the artist's inner world , inviting contemplation and a deep connection with the natural.

More information

SPIRAL JETTY

Spiral Jetty is an installation by Robert Smithson created in 1970 at Great Salt Lake, Utah. It consists of a spiral of rocks and earth that enters the water, fusing art and nature. The work reflects Smithson's interest in geology and the passage of time, symbolising the interaction between the man-made and the natural processes of erosion and transformation.

More information

THE STARRY NIGHT

The starry night shows a turbulent and energetic sky, reflecting Van Gogh's inner struggle as he seeks to find meaning in the midst of his emotional anguish, with a vibrant universe contrasting with the calm of the village below.
