Welcome

In this gallery, various interpretations of nature are displayed through art.

Here, you can enjoy a small selection of artworks with different monthly themes. This month’s theme is:


MUSIC

We invite you to reflect on the transformative power of music. Each work exhibited here is an echo of deep emotions , vibrations that connect souls beyond words. Music is not only listened to: it is felt, lived, remembered. It is refuge in chaos, comfort in pain, and celebration in joy. Through it, the artists capture the invisible: nostalgia, freedom, love and rebellion. In these creations, music is not only a theme, but a universal language that reminds us of what unites us. In a world of noise, music is still harmony.

THE CONCERT

It depicts an intimate, everyday 17th-century scene of three figures enjoying music in a domestic setting. With a baroque style and a masterful use of light, Vermeer conveys serenity, harmony and balance. The work evokes the value of music as a form of human connection and expression.

SIBELIUS MONUMENT

It represents an abstract homage to the Finnish composer Jean Sibelius. Composed of more than 600 steel pipes evoking organs, the monument suggests the strength and depth of his music. With a modern, expressionistic approach, it symbolises the connection between art, nature and sound.

More information

THE MUSICIANS

It depicts a group of young people gathered around music, reflecting joy, energy and camaraderie. With a figurative and colourful style, the work celebrates youthful vitality and the power of music as a means of union and expression. It conveys optimism and dynamism.

More information

SPIRIT OF MUSIC

It is a monument dedicated to the conductor Theodore Thomas, representing the essence of music as a transformative force. The female figure, holding a trumpet and symbolising the spirit of music, exudes a sense of movement and harmony. With a classic and stylised design, the work captures Thomas' legacy and his influence on American classical music .

More information

MUSIC IN THE TUILERIES

A work that captures the vibrant atmosphere of a Parisian garden during the 19th century. In an Impressionist style , Degas portrays a crowd enjoying music in the open air, while observing the interaction between the figures and their surroundings. The composition conveys a sense of elegance and lightness, using a soft palette of colours and highlighting everyday life in Parisian society.

More information

UNTITLED

A work that captures the dynamic and emotional essence of jazz. Through an abstract and expressionistic style, Spaulding depicts the musicians in fluid, rhythmic movement, conveying the energy and improvisation characteristic of the genre. The vibrant colour palette and fragmented forms reflect the chaos and creative freedom inherent in jazz, while the absence of a title suggests the viewer's open and personal interpretation of the music and its influence.

MUSIC

It celebrates harmony and rhythm through simple forms and bold colours. The painting features human figures that seem to dance to the music, conveying a sense of movement and fluidity. In his Fauvist style, Matisse uses flat colours and balanced compositions to evoke the energy and emotion that music can provoke. The work reflects his belief in the power of painting to express universal feelings and the connection between art and life.

MOZART

Stunning sculpture created at the 2013 International Sand Sculpture Festival in the Algarve. ProSandArt artists used sand to sculpt the figure of Mozart, capturing the essence of the classical composer in an ephemeral and detailed work. This event, which paid tribute to celebrated musicians and composers from different eras, fused classical art with modern music in a unique and creative format.

More information

THE OLD GUITARIST

It depicts an old blind man playing the guitar, immersed in poverty and loneliness. In his own style from Picasso's blue period, the work conveys melancholy, suffering and compassion. Through cold tones and elongated forms, it expresses human fragility and dignity in adversity.
