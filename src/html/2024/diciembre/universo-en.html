ASTRONOMER BY CANDLELIGHT

This Baroque painting portrays an astronomer engrossed in study by candlelight, symbolizing humanity's quest to understand the cosmos. The candle he holds provides the only source of light; it illuminates his face, the book, the precious globe, the beaker of water, and his hourglass. Strong contrasts between light and dark show the painter Gerrit Dou's knowledge of a popular Baroque technique known as chiaroscuro. Carefully rendering minute details such as the handwriting in the book, the texture of the glass flask, and the folds in the astronomer's cloak was Dou's special talent.

COSMIC DANCER

A geometric sculpture designed for zero gravity, sent to the Mir space station to explore the interaction of art and space environments. The sculpture was designed to be a three-dimensional artwork that could float freely in the microgravity environment of space, allowing astronauts to experience art in a new context.

THE CREATION OF THE SUN, MOON, AND PLANTS

Part of the Sistine Chapel ceiling, this fresco depicts God's creation of celestial bodies and vegetation, blending religious themes with cosmic elements. On the right, God is about to give shape, with his outstretched arms, to the incandescent disk of the sun and the cold one of the moon; on the other side, with an imperious gesture, he summons forth tufts of grass and the first bushes from the bare earth. SATURN AS SEEN FROM TITAN

An iconic space art painting depicting Saturn from the surface of its moon Titan, showcasing Bonestell's vision of the cosmos. This work presents an enchanting scene of Saturn from the viewpoint of its icy moon, Titan. Published in the May 29, 1944 issue of Life magazine‚Äîand thus seen by millions of Americans‚Äîthe work depicts the ringed planet suspended above a craggy landscape against a starry sky, framed by the rising rocks that gleam to reveal every detail. STARS (STERNE)

Utilizing negatives from the European Southern Observatory, Ruff created large-scale images that challenge perceptions of space and photography. The series presents the night sky in a way that questions the authenticity and objectivity of astronomical imagery, blending scientific documentation with artistic interpretation.

MATHEMATICAL SCULPTURES

Ferguson's works celebrate the intersection of mathematics and art, embodying complex geometric forms that reflect cosmic harmony. One notable piece is the torus sculpture installed at Stony Brook University, which sits on a granite base inscribed with various mathematical formulas defining the torus. The sculpture was created using a robotic arm and a diamond-encrusted cutting tool, showcasing the blend of technology, mathematics, and artistry.

INFINITY MIRROR ROOMS

These immersive environments use mirrors and lights to create illusions of infinite space, evoking the vastness of the universe. Kusama's Infinity Mirror Rooms transform spaces into endless reflections, making them perfect for the social media selfie culture. Her art reflects her life experiences and obsessions, creating a perpetual motion device of self-promotion and work that captivates audiences globally.

SKY MIRROR

Sky Mirror is an installation by Robert Smithson created in 1970 at Great Salt Lake, Utah. It consists of a spiral of rocks and earth that enters the water, fusing art and nature. The work reflects Smithson's interest in geology and the passage of time, symbolising the interaction between the man-made and the natural processes of erosion and transformation.

A UNIVERSE

A kinetic sculpture representing planetary motion, showcasing Calder's exploration of movement and balance. "A Universe" is a motorized mobile that reflects the motion of celestial bodies, embodying the harmony and mechanics of the cosmos through abstract forms and movement.
